📋How HomeBase Works

HomeBase is a household task tracker and journal built around a simple reward loop: complete tasks, earn points, hit your weekly goal, and claim rewards. Each person in your household tracks their own progress independently, but you can view everyone together.

  • Tasks are assigned to specific days of the week (recurring) or a single date (one-time). Bonus tasks are a pool of optional activities you can log on any day.
  • Points are earned by completing tasks. They accumulate through the week and reset each Sunday.
  • Weekly Goal is the point target for each person. Meeting it unlocks Goal Rewards and Credits.
  • Rewards are claimed on the Dashboard after each week closes. Point Rewards cost points. Goal Rewards require meeting the full weekly goal. Credits save up over multiple weeks toward big Credit Rewards.
  • Events & Appointments appear on the Calendar and Week view but don't affect points.
  • Diary is a private daily journal, separate from task tracking.
⭐Choosing a Point Scale

The core principle

Points should feel meaningful without being tedious to track mentally. The best approach is to anchor everything to your weekly goal — decide what a solid, full week of effort looks like, then set the goal equal to the points that effort earns.

Use 10 points as your base unit for a standard task, then scale up or down by effort:

Task TypeExamplesPoints
Quick daily habitMake bed, feed pet, wipe counter5 pts
Standard taskWash dishes, take out trash, tidy room10 pts
Bigger effortVacuum, mop, clean bathroom, fold laundry15–20 pts
Major taskDeep clean, yard work, laundry start-to-finish25–30 pts
Bonus / self-careHaircut, workout, cook a full meal, walk10–20 pts
💡 Start generous. If nobody hits their goal the first week, motivation drops fast. You can always tighten the scale once the habit is established.

Setting the weekly goal

Add up a realistic week for each person, then round to a clean number. Leave a small buffer — the goal should require consistency but not perfection.

Example calculation for a child with daily chores:

Make bed (5 pts × 7 days)35 pts
Clear dishes (10 pts × 5 days)50 pts
Vacuum bedroom (15 pts × 1)15 pts
Take out trash (10 pts × 2)20 pts
Total if perfect120 pts
Suggested goal (≈80%)100 pts

Setting the goal at 100 instead of 120 means they can miss a couple of days and still hit it — which is realistic and keeps them engaged.

🏆Scaling Rewards

Point Rewards

Price these at roughly what they're worth in effort. If a reward is enjoyable enough that a person would happily skip two days of chores to earn it, it should cost about two days' worth of points.

Reward SizeExamplesPoints
Small treatExtra screen time, late bedtime, choose dessert25–40 pts
Medium rewardChoose dinner, friend sleepover, rent a movie60–80 pts
Large rewardSpecial outing, buy a game, stay up late100–120 pts

Goal Rewards

These unlock only when the full weekly goal is met — they should feel meaningfully better than anything available from points alone. Think of them as the "bonus" for a truly solid week.

  • Family movie night of their choice
  • Trip to a favorite restaurant
  • Special activity (mini golf, bowling, arcade)
  • Extra allowance or spending money

Credits & Credit Rewards

Credits are earned one per week by meeting the goal (choose Credit instead of a Goal Reward). They accumulate over weeks toward massive rewards that wouldn't make sense as a single-week prize.

Credit CostSuggested Rewards
3 creditsNew book or small toy, extended screen day
5 creditsNew video game, theme park trip, big outing
8 creditsNew device accessory, concert, overnight trip
10+ creditsMajor purchase, vacation activity, big experience
💡 At one credit per week, 5 credits takes about 5 weeks of consistently good behavior — which makes it feel earned without being discouraging.

🏷Organizing with Categories

Regular categories

Use categories to group related tasks visually — every task in a category shares its color on the calendar and week view. Good starting categories:

  • 🏠 Cleaning — Vacuuming, mopping, bathroom, dusting
  • 🍽 Kitchen — Dishes, cooking, groceries, wiping counters
  • 🛏 Bedroom — Making bed, tidying, laundry
  • 🌱 Outdoor — Yard work, trash, recycling, gardening
  • 📚 School / Work — Homework, studying, professional tasks

Bonus categories

Mark a category as Bonus to make all its tasks on-demand — they don't appear on scheduled days and are logged manually when completed. Great for:

  • ✨ Self-Care — Haircut, workout, meditation, walk
  • 🎨 Hobbies — Practice instrument, art project, creative work
  • 🤝 Helping Out — Helping a sibling, extra task not on schedule
  • 🌟 Above & Beyond — Did something thoughtful or extra
✨Using Bonus Tasks

What they're for

Bonus tasks reward effort that doesn't fit a fixed schedule. Getting a haircut once a month, going to the gym whenever motivation strikes, or helping out with an unexpected task — these deserve recognition but don't belong in the weekly rotation.

How to log one

  1. Click ✨ Bonus Task on the Dashboard or Week View (or the small "+ bonus" button at the bottom of any day column)
  2. Pick the date it happened
  3. Choose a person (optional)
  4. Select the category, then the specific task
  5. Hit Log Task — points are added immediately
💡 Bonus tasks count toward weekly points and reward thresholds just like regular tasks — the only difference is they have no fixed schedule.
💰The Credit System

How credits work

When someone meets their weekly goal, they can choose between a Goal Reward (immediate, used this week) or a Credit (saved toward something bigger). Credits never expire and accumulate in the Rewards → Credits tab.

The tradeoff

This creates a meaningful decision each week: take something enjoyable now, or save toward something truly special. It teaches delayed gratification while keeping weekly motivation high — because hitting the goal is always worth it either way.

Claiming Credit Rewards

Go to Rewards → 💰 Credits. Each person's balance shows how many credits they have and which Credit Rewards they can afford. Click a reward to spend the credits — it saves to their Earned Rewards history.

💡 Claiming a Point Reward blocks the Goal Reward and Credit for that week — the idea is that you've already spent some of your week's earnings on a smaller prize, so the big prize is off the table. Choose wisely.
💡Tips for Getting Started

Week 1 checklist

  1. Add everyone in People with their name, color, and weekly goal
  2. Create your Categories (include at least one Bonus category)
  3. Add Tasks — start with 5–8 per person, don't overload
  4. Set up a few Rewards in each tier before the week starts
  5. Add any Events for the coming weeks

Common mistakes to avoid

  • Too many tasks: 5–8 recurring tasks per person is plenty. More than that feels overwhelming and people stop engaging.
  • Goal set too high: If the goal requires a perfect week, people give up mid-week after missing one day. Aim for 80% of a perfect week.
  • No small rewards: If the cheapest Point Reward is expensive, there's no payoff for partial effort. Always have something achievable for 30–40% of the weekly goal.
  • Forgetting bonus tasks: Set a reminder to check in at the end of each day — bonus tasks are easily forgotten if not logged same-day.

Backup your data

HomeBase saves everything to your browser's local storage (IndexedDB). This means data lives in this browser — it won't appear on another device automatically. Use the 💾 Export button in the sidebar to save a backup file, and 📂 Import on any other device to restore it.

Export regularly, especially before clearing browser data or switching devices.
